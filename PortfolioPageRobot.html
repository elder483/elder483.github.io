Overview

This was a solo two week skills development project. My primary focus at the time was working with cameras to make interesting design choices.
I created everything in the game this includes drawing title screens controls screens modelling characters, VFX audio creation and more.
I wanted to experiment with sockets, physics and camera effects, this was all a test to see how much I could achieve in a week.
The camera transition screens were inspired by the games 'Resident Evil/Biohazard' and 'Devil May Cry'.

Problems and Solutions

The Player Character

The main problem I wanted to solve with the character was utilising Unreal Engine 4's physics system and using sockets.
I decided that giving the robot a magnet hand it would allow them to pick up certain objects.
I made a very simple puzzle in which the player would pick up a block and place it on a pressure plate.

Enemies

I needed some win lose conditions the player could fall if they missed a jump and I incorporated turrets that would fire projectiles.

Story and Level Structure

I made the level fairly simplistic as I wanted to really focus on the camera cuts and techniques of following the player.
I implemented force fields to stop the player from advancing, the way to deactivate these was to acquire keys within the level.
Using the camera cuts some of the platforming sections were a little difficult due to the perspective.
However, I wanted to achieve the feeling similar to 'Crash Bandicoot' where the player would be running towards the camera.
I also wanted to experiment changing cameras at different heights and this led to jump pads once the player reaches the next level the camera would shift.
This may have created jarring visual experiences but it was a good experience.
