Overview

This was my first solo project outside of university, it spanned roughly 6 months as this was an on and off project. This was also my first attmept at a design document.
This project is a first person shooter which relies on the player to use different weapon modes to combat adversaries and navigate the level.
I handled everything from characters, levels and game design, 3D models in blender, linking animations, AI and more.
I wanted to continue my Japanese studies and integrate that into my work.
Aside from bilingual text I designed most elements on kanji i.e. the bells use 鐘 and enemies are designed with the kanji 夢.
Inspired by various games such as 'Borderlands', 'Final Fantasy' and 'The Legend of Zelda'.

Problems and Solutions

The Player Character

Movement and traversal

The initial problem I wanted to address was player movement capabilities and how I could design a level using these.
I decided to add mechanics seen in various modern shooter games such as mantles and the wall run in 'Titanfall'.
This allowed me to gradually showcase these mechanics at the beginning in a safe environment, and build up player's mastery over the course of the level.
The ability to mantle allowed the player to reach certain areas and allowed for interesting platforming sections by chaining wall jumps to mantles.

Combat

My primary challenge was to implement weapon abilities that each have their own purpose both inside and outside of combat.
I started off concepting two weapons, the gun and katana, I knew I wanted to have a maximum of four modes on each useable weapon.
My next issue was what abilities do I give them, how can they be used in and out of combat, what aboult health, are they fun to use?
Both weapons needed a primary firing/attack mode, this was the easiest to implement and the foundation that the rest were built on.
I needed to show the player what ability they had equipped but also in the environment what ability they should use.
To accomplish this I incorporated tutorials implemented by UI each time they needed to use the ability for the first time, and each ability had a colour to signify this.
Most abilities had durations and cooldowns, these needed to be implemented to balance and encourage the player to switch modes.
Each weapon would have three modes unlocked by default and the fourth would be unlocked through a quest, creating meaningful reasons to do side content.
These ultimate skills needed to be developed in a way that was interesting to use but also aren't required to finish the game.

The Gun

The gun's modes are, Shoot, Chargeshot, Barrier and Shinkansen (ultimate).
Shooting would be used as the primary form, this was used to defeat enemies, killing certain enemies would unlock the next room or a portal.
Chargeshot, like shooting it could be used to defeat enemies, however, it could also be used to destroy certain walls, signified by a red crack.
Chargeshot's damage value had a minimum and a maximum as the player could charge and expand the size of the projectile, the cooldown would be the same regardless.
Barrier was an interesting concept as I wanted a way for the player to defend themselves but also recover health, as I didn't want to implement pick-ups.
The initial tutorial for Barrier pits the player against a turret in a hallway after applying some damage and using the barrier they will see their health recover.
The next issues was how to use the Barrier outside of combat. I used the 'Lens of Truth' from 'The Legend of Zelda' to show hidden objects when the Barrier was active.
This also like the Chargeshot had a duration and cooldown.
The Shinkansen (Bullet Train in Japanese) is an ultimate ability which would have been unlocked through a quest, but was implemented and left in the final build.
I wanted to create a mechanic which when used encouraged mastery. The player fires a remote controlled projectile, which collects enemies that it hits and then detonates when a timer has expired.
The mastery element was increasing time, everytime an enemy was hit the timer would regain some time extending the abilties use.
The next issue during this ability was the player could still be hurt, so during this ability I had to set it so the player couldn't be hurt.
Due to the nature of it being an ultimate skill a long cooldown was required.
The final issue was what about bosses? The player shouldn't be able to pick up and destroy a boss in a single hit, for this using a tag to check if it was a boss or enemy it would provide a different outcome.
Instead of capturing a boss it would merely detonate and cause a large amount of damage.

The Katana

The katana's modes are, Attack, SoulShift, Counter and Dragon's Blade (ultimate).
Similarly to shooting in the gun, attacking would be the primary method for combat.
SoulShift was inspired by 'Final Fantasy IV's Dark Knight Cecil'. I wanted to add a high risk, high reward ability.
Soulshift has three tiers, to activate this ability the player would charge their sword with dark energy, sapping their health in the process.
Tier 1 provides extra damage, tier 2 adds a chance to fire a blade beam which does ranged damage and extra damage, Tier 3 more damage and always fires beams.
The player must switch from soulshift back to attack to use this abilities properties. If the player has low health they can use SoulShift to enter tier 3.
The player can't attack whilst charging and will walk slowly. Although not implemented one concept was to have certain doors open when the player's health was low enough.
Counter would act differently depending on the type of attack recieved. Firstly if hit by melee the player would knockback and strike the instigator.
If the attack was a ranged projectile they would teleport to the enemies location and deal a counterattack.
I used a healing turret to showcase the counter ability for the first time allowing the player to warp to progress.
Dragon's Blade originally was to be unlocked via a quest. This ability would have a long cooldown but essentially incorporated all three of the other abilities into one with none of the negatives.
Tier 3 Soulshift an auto counter and the attack ability all in one ability.

Enemies and Bosses

I needed to provide the player with enemies to utilise all of these abilities. Two enemies and turrets all provide similar functionality.
Bosses however, would be more complex. The first boss the Ronin gives the player the katana at the start and the battle won't initiate until the weapon is equipped.
If the player shoots the Ronin then he will reflect/parry the bullets encouraging the player to switch.
In the gallery section you can see the bosses functionality through gifs and videos.

Story and Level Structure

The level was split into four main sections, the Tutorial section, the Garden, the Bridge and the Tower.
The game would introduce new mechanics as and when they were required to progress.
The Ronin boss was used as a way to challenge the player and throw them in the deep end with the katana.
After this the katana's skills such as counter was taught more efficiently in the tower.

Synopsis/Demo

In this demo the player must make use of their combat abilities both in battle and to escape the Dreamscape.
The player has two weapons with four modes to utilise.
By defeating the bosses new paths will open allowing you to complete your mission.
